Area Forecast Discussion
National Weather Service State College PA
209 PM EDT Tue Jun 21 2022

.SYNOPSIS...
Summertime heat and humidity arriving on schedule will increase
into midweek, fueling a risk of severe thunderstorms and
flooding downpours on Wednesday. Showers could linger over
eastern Pennsylvania early Thursday, but the overall
precipitation trend will be drying out with lower humidity for
late week. The last weekend of June 2022 should start off dry
before showers and thunderstorms likely return on Sunday.

&&

.NEAR TERM /THROUGH TONIGHT/...
Soggy morning for some is a distant memory as widesapread
sunshine and warmth coversall but the far eastern zones.
Lingering stratocumulus over portions of Luzerne and Schulkill
Counties will be a bit slower to push off to the east, but
expect this to happen after 19z. Elsewhere, expect partial to
full sunshine and temperatures climbing through the 80s with
moderate humidity.

Warm front becomes stationary just to my northeast tonight with
central PA in the warm sector. Deep layer moisture on the high
side with slight shower chances invof the front extending into
the central mountains. It will become mostly cloudy as a result,
with mins on the mild side...ranging from the upper 60s to
around 70 over much of the west to the lower 60s east.

&&

.SHORT TERM /WEDNESDAY THROUGH WEDNESDAY NIGHT/...
Wednesday continues to shape up to be a potentially active wx
day across CPA with both WPC and SPC upgrading the ERO and SWO
categorical risk levels from MRGL to SLGT. A shortwave trough
is expected to progress through the northern periphery of
aforementioned upper ridge, sending an attendant cold front
southeastward across the OH Valley and the Lower Great Lakes.
Downstream of this shortwave, an upper low is forecast to begin
the period off the Northeast coast, before gradually drifting
westward towards southern New England.

A very warm and moist/unstable airmass will likely be in place
ahead of the cold front particularly across west-central PA. A
sfc trough/convergence boundary sets up across the area (just
behind warm front over EPA) and should act as a focusing
mechanism for t-storm development. The thermodynamic conditions
combined with interacting sfc features and enhanced mid-level
flow around the upper low off the Northeast coast will support
the potential for isolated strong to severe t-storms during the
afternoon and evening. Damaging winds are the primary hazard,
but will also need to be cognizant of an isolated tornado risk
near the sfc trough/warm frontal boundaries positioned closer
to stronger vertical shear profiles over the eastern half to
1/3 of the area. SLGT risk ERO was added to east-central PA
given potential for training storms in a north to south
direction in the vicinity of the sfc front. PWs are fcst in the
1.5 to 2+ standard deviation above the mean range and 12hr HREF
neighborhood probs for 1-2"+ amounts ending 00Z Thu are quite
high at 40-90 and 30% respectively. Limiting factor for flooding
will be very high FFGs due to below normal rainfall over the
past 14 days, but training/enhanced rain rates in the right
location (urban areas) could still lead to a few instances of
flooding. MaxTs peak in the 80-90F range on Wednesday.

&&

.LONG TERM /THURSDAY THROUGH MONDAY/...
Cold front still not quite through the CWA at 12Z Thursday, and
a weak upper ridge will be overhead. That will result in some
lingering showers over the eastern part/half of the CWA on
Thursday, at least in the morning. They could push east of the
CWA in the early aftn, but will leave a tiny PoP in the E for
the late aftn.

Pattern then becomes a confluent/NW upper level flow and a sfc
high will build in. Fri looks dry with seasonable temps for
sure. Then, temps rise for the rest of the weekend. Some L90s
are expected in the SErn valleys/metro areas on Sun. Llvl
heating may lead to enough instability to pop an isolated
SHRA/TSRA near the MD border Sat aftn. But, the poss is so low
that I`ll only drop a 20 PoP there.

Synoptic storm develops over high plains and moves eastward
through the weekend. At this point, the warm front may hang up
just to the east of the CWA on Sunday. and the cold front will
catch up to it late in day and overnight (Mon AM). This will
create typical showers and thunderstorms Sunday, peaking in the
late day and evening hours, perhaps lasting for a good part of
the night in the east. Timing has been rather consistent thus
far over the last few days. But, timing is always tenuous when
talking about day 5.

Some lingering convection possible (SE) Monday morning,
depending on timing of the front. But, most places should be
drying out on Monday, and dry wx should then last into the
middle of the week.

&&

.AVIATION /18Z TUESDAY THROUGH SUNDAY/...
18Z TAFS sent. After some elevated convection this morning,
a nice afternoon. Moisture at low levels to the west of the
front is not very deep, so not looking at much potential
for clouds this afternoon, or lower clouds and fog late
tonight.

For Wednesday, only went with VCSH in the TAF package for
now, maybe more potential for storms late Wed. However, the
NAM model rather dry, which may be due to winds shifting
more to the west across much of the area early on. Hard
to line up parameters at this point, went through this
for last Wednesday night. Large scale weather pattern is
similar to last week.

Likewise, some spread in the guidance for late week. Will
weight the outlook toward the dry side for now.

Outlook...

Thu...Mainly VFR. Isolated SHRA SE.

Fri-Sat...Mainly VFR.

Sun...Mainly VFR. Chance of a shower or storm, mainly late.
